## Description

The PCM5102A is a 32-bit, 384 kHz stereo audio DAC from Texas Instruments featuring an integrated line-level output driver with DirectPath technology, which provides a ground-centered output that eliminates the need for DC-blocking capacitors. It includes a high-performance audio PLL that generates the system clock internally from the BCK reference, removing the requirement for an external master clock (SCK). The device accepts I2S or left-justified audio data in 16-, 24-, or 32-bit formats and is configured entirely via hardware pins.

## Application Notes

- **I2S interface:** The PCM5102A accepts standard I2S or left-justified PCM audio data via three signals: BCK (bit clock), DIN (serial data), and LRCK (left/right word clock). No master clock (SCK) is required when using the internal PLL -- connect SCK directly to GND. The internal PLL locks to BCK and generates all required internal clocks. Supported BCK-to-LRCK ratios are 32, 48, and 64 per channel (i.e., 64, 96, or 128 BCK cycles per LRCK frame). The device accepts 16-, 24-, and 32-bit audio words.
- **Audio format config pins:** FMT selects between I2S format (LOW) and left-justified format (HIGH). FLT selects the digital interpolation filter: normal latency (LOW) provides a sharp roll-off filter, while low latency (HIGH) provides a slow roll-off filter with reduced group delay. DEMP enables 44.1 kHz digital de-emphasis when HIGH. XSMT controls the soft mute function: drive HIGH for normal operation, LOW to engage a soft mute ramp that achieves 120 dB of mute attenuation. On power-up, XSMT should be held LOW until clocks are stable, then driven HIGH to unmute.
- **Power supply:** The device requires three supply rails: AVDD (3.3 V analog), DVDD (1.8 V or 3.3 V digital), and CPVDD (3.3 V charge pump). When DVDD is supplied at 3.3 V, the internal LDO regulates it down to 1.8 V for the digital core -- the LDO output (LDOO, pin 20) must be decoupled with a 1 uF ceramic capacitor. AVDD and CPVDD should share the same 3.3 V supply but be decoupled independently.
- **Decoupling:** Place 10 uF + 100 nF ceramic capacitors on AVDD (pin 18) and CPVDD (pin 12), each as close to the pin as possible. Place a 1 uF ceramic capacitor on LDOO (pin 20). Place a 100 nF ceramic capacitor on DVDD (pin 19). Decouple VNEG (pin 14) with a 1 uF ceramic capacitor to CPGND.
- **Charge pump capacitors:** Connect a 220 nF to 1 uF ceramic capacitor between CAPP (pin 11) and CAPM (pin 13). This flying capacitor is used by the internal charge pump to generate the negative supply rail (VNEG) for the ground-centered output stage.
- **Output stage:** The DirectPath output eliminates the need for external DC-blocking capacitors. OUTL and OUTR provide a ground-centered 2.1 VRMS full-scale signal that can drive loads down to 1 kohm directly. For best performance, keep the output traces short and route them away from digital signals. A series resistor (e.g., 470 ohm) can be used to form a low-pass filter with the cable capacitance for EMI filtering.
- **Grounding:** Maintain separate analog ground (AGND) and digital ground planes. Connect AGND (pins 9 and 16) and CPGND (pin 10) together at a single star point near the device. Tie digital ground to analog ground at the same star point.
