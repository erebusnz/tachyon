## Description

The OPA1642 is a dual JFET-input, rail-to-rail-output operational amplifier from Texas Instruments' SoundPlus family, designed for ultralow distortion (0.00005% THD+N at 1 kHz) and low noise (5.1 nV/sqrt(Hz) at 1 kHz). In this design it serves as the precision CV output buffer for the DAC8552, configured as a non-inverting amplifier with x4 gain on ±12 V rails to produce 0--10 V unipolar 1 V/oct CV outputs. Its JFET input stage provides sub-picoamp input bias current, eliminating DC error from feedback resistor bias current drops, and its rail-to-rail output stage allows the output to swing within 350 mV of either supply rail under a 2 kohm load.

## Application Notes

- **TL072 pin-compatible drop-in.** The OPA1642 in SOIC-8 uses the exact same standard dual op-amp pinout as the TL072 SOIC-8 (OUT A / -IN A / +IN A / V- / +IN B / -IN B / OUT B / V+). It can replace a TL072 on an existing board footprint without layout changes, delivering lower noise, far lower input bias current, and rail-to-rail output swing as upgrades.

- **Rail-to-rail output headroom at ±12 V.** The datasheet specifies the output can swing to within 0.35 V of each rail with a 2 kohm load, and within 0.20 V with a 10 kohm load. At ±12 V supplies, the output can reach approximately +11.65 V and -11.65 V into 2 kohm, and approximately +11.80 V / -11.80 V into 10 kohm. For the 0--10 V CV output application with a light downstream load (typically > 10 kohm Eurorack input impedance), the output comfortably covers the full 0--10 V range with margin on both rails.

- **Supply range and ±12 V margin.** The OPA1642 is specified for dual supplies from ±2.25 V to ±18 V (absolute maximum 40 V total, i.e. ±20 V). Operation at ±12 V is well within specification, sitting at the midpoint of the usable range with a comfortable 6 V margin below the ±18 V maximum.

- **Input offset voltage and pitch calibration.** Vos is 1 mV typical and 3.5 mV maximum at TA = 25 deg C, with a PSRR of 0.14 uV/V typical and 2 uV/V maximum. For a x4 non-inverting gain stage, a 3.5 mV worst-case input offset appears as up to 14 mV of output offset error. At the standard 1 V/oct scale this equates to approximately 14 cents of pitch error at most, which is within the calibration range of any standard pitch calibration routine. The offset has negligible temperature dependence compared to resistor tolerances and should be cancelled by the firmware calibration procedure.

- **No phase reversal.** The OPA1642 has internal phase-reversal protection. If the input is driven beyond the common-mode range (V- - 0.1 V to V+ - 3.5 V), the output clamps to the appropriate rail rather than reversing phase. This is important during power sequencing when the DAC output may transiently exceed the amplifier's input range.

- **Input common-mode range note.** The input common-mode range extends to within 3.5 V of V+. With ±12 V supplies, the upper CM limit is approximately +8.5 V. The non-inverting x4 gain configuration keeps the input well below this limit (maximum DAC output is 2.5 V into a 2.5 V reference), so this constraint is not a concern in this design.

- **Capacitive load stability (Eurorack cables).** The OPA1642 is unity-gain stable but is sensitive to capacitive loads at the output. The datasheet (Section 8.1.5) states that large capacitive loads reduce phase margin and can cause gain peaking or oscillation, and recommends adding a small series resistor (ROUT = 50 ohm, for example) between the op-amp output and the load. Eurorack patch cables are approximately 100--200 pF per metre, which is a meaningful capacitive load at 11 MHz GBW. The small-signal overshoot curves (Figures 19 and 20) show that with ROUT = 0 ohm the overshoot at 100 pF is roughly 25--35%; a 51 ohm series resistor reduces this to under 10%. A 47--100 ohm series resistor in series with each CV output is strongly recommended for Eurorack compatibility.

- **Power supply decoupling.** Place 100 nF low-ESR ceramic bypass capacitors from V+ (pin 8) to GND and from V- (pin 4) to GND, as close to the device as physically possible. Add 10 uF electrolytic capacitors in parallel for low-frequency supply noise rejection. Keep input traces short and routed away from supply and output traces.

- **Thermal protection.** The OPA1642 includes an internal thermal shutdown circuit that activates at approximately 180 deg C junction temperature (with 15 deg C hysteresis). At ±12 V with normal audio loads this will not be approached, but note that a sustained output short-circuit at ±12 V can dissipate significant power in the SOIC-8 package (RθJA = 160 deg C/W for the D package).
