## Description

The REF5025 is a low-noise, low-drift, precision 2.5V voltage reference from the REF50xx family by Texas Instruments. It features an integrated heater circuit for excellent thermal stability, providing initial accuracy of 0.05% (max) and a temperature drift of 3 ppm/degC (max) in the standard-grade device. It is capable of sourcing and sinking up to 10 mA of output current, making it suitable for driving ADC and DAC reference inputs.

## Application Notes

- **Decoupling**: Place a 0.1 uF ceramic capacitor close to the VIN pin. A 1 uF to 10 uF ceramic or tantalum capacitor is recommended on VOUT for stability and transient response. The output is stable with any capacitive load.
- **Noise reduction pin (TRIM/NR)**: Connect an external capacitor (typically 0.1 uF to 1 uF) from pin 2 (TRIM/NR) to GND to reduce broadband output noise. Larger capacitors yield lower noise but slower startup.
- **Enable pin**: The EN pin (pin 8) is active-low. To enable the output, tie EN to GND or leave it floating (internal pull-down). To disable, drive EN high (to VIN).
- **Minimum input headroom**: The input voltage must be at least VOUT + 0.5V (i.e., 3.0V minimum for the REF5025) for proper regulation.
- **Load current**: The output can source up to +10 mA and sink up to -10 mA. Exceeding these limits degrades accuracy and may damage the device.
- **Thermal considerations**: The integrated temperature sensor on pin 3 outputs a voltage proportional to die temperature (~2 mV/degC) and can be used for system-level thermal compensation.
- **Long-term stability**: The SOIC-8 package provides better long-term drift (22 ppm/1000 hrs) compared to the VSSOP-8 package (50 ppm/1000 hrs).
