## Description

The REF5025 is a low-noise, low-drift, precision 2.5V voltage reference from the REF50xx family by Texas Instruments. The standard-grade REF5025IDR (I-suffix) provides 0.1% initial accuracy (max) and 8 ppm/degC temperature drift (max). The higher-grade REF5025AIDR (AI-suffix) tightens these to 0.05% and 3 ppm/degC. It is capable of sourcing and sinking up to 10 mA of output current, making it suitable for driving ADC and DAC reference inputs.

## Application Notes

- **Decoupling**: Place a 0.1 uF ceramic capacitor close to the VIN pin (pin 2). A 1 uF to 10 uF ceramic or tantalum capacitor is recommended on VOUT (pin 6) for stability and transient response. The output is stable with any capacitive load.
- **Noise reduction pin (TRIM/NR)**: Connect an external capacitor (typically 0.1 uF to 1 uF) from pin 5 (TRIM/NR) to GND to reduce broadband output noise. Larger capacitors yield lower noise but slower startup.
- **DNC pins**: Pins 1 and 8 are Do-Not-Connect. Leave them floating; do not tie to any net (this package variant has no EN pin).
- **Minimum input headroom**: VS must be at least VOUT + 0.2 V; for the REF5025 specifically the datasheet floor is **2.7 V** (footnote (1) on §6.5 Power Supply row).
- **Load current**: The output can source up to +10 mA and sink up to -10 mA. Exceeding these limits degrades accuracy and may damage the device.
- **Thermal considerations**: The integrated temperature sensor on pin 3 outputs a voltage proportional to die temperature (~2 mV/degC) and can be used for system-level thermal compensation.
- **Long-term stability**: The SOIC-8 package provides better long-term drift (22 ppm/1000 hrs) compared to the VSSOP-8 package (50 ppm/1000 hrs).
