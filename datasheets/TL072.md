## Description

The TL072 is a dual low-noise JFET-input operational amplifier from Texas Instruments, offering high input impedance, low noise (37 nV/sqrt(Hz) typical at 1 kHz), and low harmonic distortion (0.003% typical). It features a 3 MHz gain-bandwidth product, 13 V/us slew rate, and internal short-circuit protection, making it well suited for audio signal conditioning, active filtering, and precision instrumentation applications. The TL072C is the commercial temperature grade rated for 0 deg C to 70 deg C operation.

## Application Notes

- **JFET input characteristics:** The JFET input stage provides extremely high input impedance (approximately 10^12 ohm) and very low input bias current (65 pA typical), making the TL072 suitable for high-impedance source circuits such as piezoelectric sensors, photodiode amplifiers, and capacitive microphone preamps where bipolar-input op-amps would introduce unacceptable loading or DC errors.
- **Power supply decoupling:** Place 100 nF ceramic bypass capacitors as close as physically possible to pins 4 (VCC-) and 8 (VCC+), with short traces to the ground plane. For audio applications or circuits sensitive to low-frequency supply noise, add 10 uF electrolytic capacitors in parallel with the ceramics on each supply rail.
- **Latch-up considerations:** Unlike CMOS op-amps, the JFET/bipolar TL072 is inherently immune to CMOS-type latch-up. However, the internal JFET input transistors can exhibit phase reversal if the input voltage is driven beyond the common-mode range toward the negative supply. This can cause the output to latch to the opposite rail. Ensure inputs remain within the specified common-mode range during all operating conditions including transients.
- **Input common-mode range limits:** The input common-mode voltage range extends from VCC- + 4 V up to VCC+. With +/-15 V supplies, this means the valid input range is approximately -11 V to +15 V. Exceeding the lower limit (approaching VCC- + 4 V or below) causes the JFET input stage to lose control of the output, potentially resulting in output phase reversal. This asymmetric range must be accounted for in AC-coupled and single-supply designs.
